Day 1-2 Seattle

Day 1: Arriving day

If you arrive in Seattle by plane at Seattle-Tacoma Airport you can easily rent a car at the SeaTac Rental Car Facility which can be reached by a free shuttle bus. The facility is a large parking garage with many desks of different rental car facilities. When you eventually rented a car, you can bound north in the direction of Seattle. On your way to downtown Seattle, you will come along Boeing Field where Boeing aircraft are assembled, which is amazing to visit. If you arrive early in Seattle, you can choose to already take a look in the city and visit one of the main attractions in Seattle, such as: Pike Place Market, the Seattle Great Wheel at Pier 57 and the Seattle Aquarium at Pier 59. Seattle is also home to the first Amazon Go shop, which is located next to the Amazon headquarters.

Where to stay in Seattle?

Tip: If you are making a road trip it is important to select an accommodation which offers the possibility to park your car nearby. Here you can find some nice accommodations with parking space close by.
  • Youth: Green Tortoise Hostel Seattle is one of the best-reviewed hostels in Seattle, with a very good location. Nearby the hostel is public parking available.
  • Affordable: La Quinta Inn & Suites by Wyndham Seattle Downtown has an excellent location and a fine price, parking spots are available.
  • Family: Stay Alfred on Western Avenue is a perfect family accommodation, with up to 6 people apartments, an indoor swimming pool, gym and even an indoor basketball court. Public parking is available near the hotel.
  • High-end: The Four Seasons Hotel Seattle is the most high-end hotel in Seattle. Some of the rooms offer a perfect view on the skyline of Seattle. The most amazing amenity of this place would be the rooftop swimming pool. 

Day 2: Exploring Seattle

View on Seattle

At the second and last day in Seattle, you need to choose what other things you want to see of the city because this might be your only chance. Next to the previously mentioned highlights other nice places to visit in Seattle are the Space Needle observation deck, the first Starbucks in the world (close by Pike Place Market) and Kerry Park, where you have an amazing view over the skyline of the city.

Day 3-4 Vancouver

Day 3: Trip to Vancouver

The city of Vancouver is widely known as one of the best cities in the world to live in. The city offers everything nature lovers want within a few hours drive. West of the city, the sea and Vancouver Island and in the east the Rocky Mountains for perfect summer and winter outdoor-activities such as mountain biking, hiking, skiing and snowboarding. The city itself is a very lovely place with amazing restaurants in Gastown and a great shopping area in Granville Street. Even if you want to escape from the bustle of the city, Vancouver is the place to be with Stanley Park on a just 30-min walk from Granville Street.

Where to stay in Vancouver?

  • Youth: C&N Backpackers Hostel Vancouver a good and decent hostel in Vancouver and offers free parking close to the accommodation. Please note that you need to make a reservation for parking.
  • Affordable: Moda Hotel is a 3-star budget hotel at an excellent location, close to Granville Street. Parking is possible near the hotel at a charge of 18 CAD per day.
  • Family: Landis Hotel & Suites offers suites with place for up to 6 people. There is also an indoor indoor swimming pool available, perfect for people that travel with children. Parking is available at additional costs.
  • High-end: Fairmont Pacific Rim is a very luxe hotel with sun terrace and swimming pool close to downtown Vancouver. Parking on site is available for an extra charge of 55 CAD per dag.

Day 4: Exploring Vancouver

This day is to visit the highlights you haven’t seen yet. Other attractions that you may want to check-out are Capilano Suspension Bridge Park, Vancouver Aquarium and the Science World Museum.

Day 5-6 Vancouver Island

Day 5: Ferry to the Island and hiking in the afternoon

It would be wise to take an early boat to Vancouver Island, by doing this it is smart to pre-book a place at the boat you want to take, so you do not waste your time by waiting for an empty spot. A reservation can be made here, website BC Ferries. When arriving at the port of Nanaimo, you have multiple options to enjoy some nature. Close to Nanaimo there are some really nice places to take a walk that do not take too much time, since you do not have that long. One of them is MacMillan Provincial Park, a small-sized park with some giant Douglas fir trees. This is a perfect park for families with small kids and people that prefer small hikes, but its definitely worth a visit. If you prefer to make a somewhat larger hike, then you should visit Little Qualicum Falls Provincial Park. This park offers longer and more challenging routes and some amazing falls. If you are only in for visiting just some falls and don’t want to walk that much, you should give Englishman River Falls Provincial Park a chance. The falls can be reached by a walk of 400 or 600 meters (depending on which one you want to visit).

After you had one or multiple hikes, it’s time to have drive to your hotel so you will be fit for wake-up early again for your visit the southernmost city of the island, Victoria.  

Where to stay at Vancouver Island (Victoria)?

  • Youth: Ocean Island Inn Backpackers Suites is probably one of the most comfortable backpackers hostels in Victoria. Parking is available near the hostel. 
  • Affordable: Marketa’s Bed & Breakfast is a very nice and affordable Bed & Breakfast near the city centre of Victoria. The accommodation offers free on-site parking. 
  • Family: Red Lion Inn and Suites Victoria is a perfect family hotel with large rooms and suites. Free parking is available on site. 
  • High-end: The Magnolia Hotel & Spa is one of the most, or maybe the most, luxury hotel on the entire island. The hotel is located in the beautiful Victoria. Valet parking is available on site. 

Day 6: Exploring Victoria

Despite the fact that Victoria is not that large, it is a very nice and vibrant place, especially with good weather. Because of this and that you will need to get the ferry at the end of the day to Port Angeles, you should get up early again. While Victoria is situated in North America, it does look very European because of its architecture. Actually, this is not very strange because the English immigrant-architecture Francis Rattenbury designed numerous of buildings in the city. He designed, for example, the British Columbia Parliament Buildings, Empress Hotel and Victoria’s Inner Harbour.

At the end of the day it is time to take the ferry to Port Angeles, back to the United States. A reservation for this ferry can be made here, website Black Ball Ferry. The next day, a visit to the city Astoria is planned, so you better drive a bit and stay in a place that is already on your way to Astoria.

Where to stay on the way between Port Angeles and Astoria?

  • Youth: Pitchwood Inn & Alehouse is a perfect choice for youngsters that would like to have an accommodation where they can drink a local-beer before going to sleep. Parking is free and there is a bunk-room available for low-budgeters.
  • Affordable: Guesthouse Montesano in Montesano is an affordable pit stop hotel with enough amenities to get you through the night. 
  • Family: Stay Beyond Inn & Suites is a nice and family-friendly accommodation with free parking. It’s on the way between Port Angeles and Astoria. 
  • High-end: Little Creek Casino Resort in Shelton might be a good choice if you are looking for more luxury. This accommodation has a golf course, casino and indoor pool to make it as comfortable as possible. Parking is available at no additional costs. 
Day 7-8 Astoria & Cannon Beach

Day 7: Exploring Astoria

This day, you are probably not in Astoria yet. This means that you need to drive a bit to reach it. That’s not that big of a problem because Astoria has actually only two spots that are worth a visit, the Astoria-Megler Bridge and the Astoria Column. The first called is the longest continuous truss bridge in North America while the second one is a tower with an observation deck that offers an amazing view over the area. After this visit, it’s time to drive to your hotel, have a nice dinner and prepare yourself for some relaxing time at Cannon Beach the next day.

Where to stay near Cannon Beach?

Day 8: Relaxing at Cannon beach

After travelling for a week, you will probably need some time to relax. Luckily you are in the right place for that, at Cannon beach, which is one of the most beautiful beaches on the west coast of the United States. The beach is characterised by Haystack Rock, where puffins can also be spotted at certain times of the year. If you visit Cannon beach during the summer you will probably choose to sunbathe and take a dip in the pacific ocean. If you are there with bad weather conditions then a walk on the beautiful beach is definitely recommended. If you are done with visiting Cannon beach, you can already drive to Portland so you have the next morning already time to spent in the city. If you can’t get enough of the beach, then drive the next morning, the drive to Portland only takes about one and a half hour.

Day 9-10 Portland

Day 9: Arriving in Portland

Whether you arrive in Portland this day or you already arrived the day before, you will most probably visit the highlights of this relaxed city. Portland has many interesting places to visit, among which the Hawthorne Bridge, the Japanese Garden and Powell’s City of Books. The last called is the largest independent book store in the world and definitely recommended to visit. The bookstore is located at Burnside Street in Portland and the store claims to have about 4 million(!) books, from mainstream to rare and from used to new. Portland has also many good places to get some food, like for example ‘The Fireside’ at NW 23th Avenue or one of the cosy at NE Alberta St (like ‘Urdaneta’ and ‘Bollywood Theater’).

Day 11-12 Painted Hills and Hells Canyon

Day 11: Visiting Painted Hills

As mentioned earlier, you better get out of bed early so you are able to visit this beautiful sight when it’s not that hot yet. Near to the Painted Hills overlook, you can find a picnic area which is a perfect spot to eat breakfast and enjoy the view! It is an amazing place to visit but please note that there are not many shops in the area, so it is recommended to buy food and drinks at the supermarket in Prineville, from here it is about an hour drive to Painted Hills. If you are done with visiting the Hills you can drive to your hotel on the way to Hells Canyon. One of the best places to spend the night and have some dinner would be Baker City, which also has a large supermarket.

Day 13-14 Shoshone Falls & Teton National Park

Day 13: Shoshone Falls (Twin Falls) 

Day 13 is probably not one of the most exciting days of this trip because you will drive all the way from Boise to Grand Teton National Park. Despite the fact that you will be most of the time on the road, there is one of the most stunning spots of this trip along the way. This spot is the Shoshone Falls in Twin Falls, a beautiful widespread waterfall which is only 2 hours from Boise. After visiting this beautiful sight, it’s time to proceed your journey to your final destination of this day Grand Teton National Park. Don’t leave too late from Boise so you will have enough time to make some stops along the road, safety is everything :)
Fact: Did you know that Shoshone Falls is named after the Native American tribe that used to live in this area?

Day 14: Grand Teton National Park

Grand Teton National Park, in the northwest of Wyoming, is a very populair but less-known national park in the United States. The park receives many visitors because it is directly south of the famous Yellowstone National Park. However, the park has many things to offer across its 480 squared miles. The park mainly exists out of the Teton Mountain Range and the Jackson Hole valley. Many fascinating sights can be found in the park, among which: Teton Peak, Jenny Lake and the Molton Barn.

Day 15-16 Yellowstone National Park & Virginia City

Day 15: Yellowstone National Park

Yellowstone National Park is one of the most famous national parks in the United States. The park has an area of almost 3,500 squared miles with a large number of different animals in the area. You will probably spot bisons, elks and bighorn sheep and if you’re lucky you may also spot wolfs, grizzly bears and lynx’s. In addition, the park is famous for its various types of scenery, with large forests, canyons, plains and geysers, of which is the Old Faithful the most visited and famous one. Next to the Old Faithful, other famous spots are the Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone, Mammoth Hot Springs and Yellowstone Lake.

Frequently asked questions (FAQ) about this trip

Is there an entrance fee for Yellowstone National Park? 
Yes, an entrance fee has to be paid when entering the park. However, there are some fee-free days each year. More information can be found here, website Yellowstone National Park.
Is it easy to cross the border between the US and Canada?

Yes, there will be a passport check on both sides of the border but you can stay in the car and hand over all passports to the immigration officer.
